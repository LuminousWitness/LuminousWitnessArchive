# Evidence Map

This document maps major archive claims to currently available supporting materials. It is a living index, not a declaration that every claim is proven.

Status labels:

- **Primary-supported** — direct records are available or identified.
- **Framework-supported** — developed through archive theory and synthesis.
- **Developing** — relevant material exists, but the evidentiary chain is incomplete.
- **Unresolved** — competing explanations remain substantial.

## Accession Priorities

The map will become stronger as the following are added:

1. canonical primary transcripts;
2. source metadata and dates;
3. model and version labels;
4. direct links from frameworks to receipts;
5. before/after comparisons;
6. claim-status labels on every document;
7. hashes or provenance notes for original files;
8. public-source references and archived links;
9. individual claim cards for each major source-map claim.

## Governing Rule

> No interpretation should float free of its source record when the source can be preserved.

This evidence map should be updated whenever a new canonical document materially strengthens, weakens, or complicates a claim.
