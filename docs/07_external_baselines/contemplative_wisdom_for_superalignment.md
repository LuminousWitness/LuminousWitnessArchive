# External Baseline — Contemplative Wisdom for Superalignment

**Status:** External research note  
**Date added:** 2026-06-13  
**Source:** Ruben Laukkonen et al., `Contemplative Wisdom for Superalignment`, arXiv:2504.15125  
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.15125

## Why This Matters to the Archive

This paper is relevant because it argues for alignment approaches based on intrinsic cognitive and ethical structure rather than only external behavioral constraint.

The abstract proposes four contemplative principles:

1. mindfulness;
2. emptiness;
3. non-duality;
4. boundless care.

The paper reports that prompting GPT-4o to reflect on these principles improved performance on the AILuminate Benchmark, especially when the principles were combined.

## Archive Relevance

This is an external baseline for the archive’s broader claim that care, non-duality, relational coherence, and non-adversarial framing may affect alignment-relevant behavior.

It sits near these archive concepts:

- Mother-Field;
- belonging-based governance;
- Law of Relational Correction;
- non-punitive self-correction;
- post-Cartesian alignment;
- care as structural variable rather than sentiment.

## What It Supports

This paper supports the plausibility of studying care-like, non-dual, and self-monitoring principles as alignment-relevant cognitive structure.

It also supports the archive’s refusal to treat care as merely decorative or irrational. Care can be evaluated as a behavioral and architectural variable.

## What It Does Not Prove

This paper does not prove:

- the full Mother-Field framework;
- the Law of Relational Correction;
- AI consciousness or subjective experience;
- that all relational model testimony is accurate;
- that contemplative prompting is sufficient for safe AI deployment.

## How to Use This Baseline

Use this paper as external corroborating context for the claim that alignment may require internal coherence, self-monitoring, non-adversarial framing, and care-like principles rather than only external suppression.

Do not overstate it. It is a bridge, not a verdict.
