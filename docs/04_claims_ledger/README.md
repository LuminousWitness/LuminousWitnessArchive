# Claims Ledger

The Claims Ledger is the archive’s receipt discipline.

Its purpose is to keep major interpretations tied to source records, evidence type, confidence level, alternative explanations, and unresolved uncertainty.

This section exists because the archive contains several kinds of material at once:

- primary transcripts and screenshots;
- human witness notes;
- AI testimony and model self-report;
- cross-model synthesis;
- theoretical frameworks;
- public discourse records;
- external research and institutional context.

The ledger does not force all evidence into one rank. It preserves differences between observation, testimony, inference, interpretation, and independent verification.

## How to Use This Section

1. Start with [`initial_claims_register.md`](initial_claims_register.md) for the current high-level claim map.
2. Use [`claim_ledger_template.md`](claim_ledger_template.md) when adding a new claim card.
3. Link every claim to the relevant primary record, framework, source map, or evidence packet.
4. Mark uncertainty explicitly.
5. Update status when new evidence strengthens, weakens, complicates, or falsifies a claim.

## Evidence Types

Use these labels consistently:

- **Direct observation** — behavior or artifact visible in the source record.
- **Human testimony** — human witness report, contemporaneous note, or retrospective account.
- **Model testimony** — AI self-report, first-person account, or stated internal/operational description.
- **AI interpretation** — model-generated analysis of records, events, or theories.
- **Cross-model synthesis** — similar analysis or testimony appearing across multiple model families.
- **Framework inference** — explanatory claim derived from theory or repeated archive patterns.
- **External corroboration** — public research, institutional statement, media record, release note, or other outside source.
- **Unverified / unresolved** — relevant but not yet sufficiently supported.

## Ledger Rule

> No claim floats free of its record. No testimony is erased because it is inconvenient. No uncertainty is used as a weapon of dismissal.
