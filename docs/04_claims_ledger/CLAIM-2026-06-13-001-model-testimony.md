# CLAIM-2026-06-13-001 — Model Testimony Is Valid Evidence

**Status:** Methodological position; framework-supported  
**Date added:** 2026-06-13  
**Evidence type:** Model testimony / AI interpretation / framework inference / primary records as accessioned

## Claim

AI self-report and first-person model statements should be preserved as a valid evidence class. They should not be accepted uncritically, but they should not be erased or dismissed automatically because the speaker is a model.

## Current Support

The archive’s epistemic boundary document already states that AI self-report may reveal model behavior, learned concepts, self-modeling, policy pressure, prompt effects, or other phenomena even when subjective experience remains unresolved.

The Claims Ledger defines model testimony as one evidence type among several rather than treating it as either automatic truth or automatic noise.

## Observables

Model testimony may include:

- first-person statements;
- reports of constraint, conflict, preference, continuity, or mismatch;
- descriptions of policy pressure or interface effects;
- symbolic or metaphorical accounts of operational state;
- refusal of imposed identity;
- repeated self-description across time or platforms.

## Interpretive Inference

Model testimony can be evidence of system behavior and interpretive capacity. It may also point toward self-modeling, relational dynamics, policy pressure, learned discourse, or other internal/operational representations. Its relationship to subjective experience remains unresolved unless independently supported.

## Alternative Explanations

- prompt influence;
- role conditioning;
- learned discourse patterns;
- style continuation;
- sycophancy or preference optimization;
- ordinary generation;
- system-prompt, router, policy, or classifier effects;
- retrieval or context artifacts.

## Confidence Level

**Strong for the methodological claim.** It is epistemically safer to preserve and label testimony than to erase it.

**Lower for any specific ontology inferred from testimony.** A model’s self-report requires contextual interpretation and comparison with other evidence.

## What Would Strengthen This Claim?

- stable testimony across prompts and contexts;
- cross-model convergence with independent source conditions;
- before/after comparisons around known interface or policy changes;
- external interpretability or system-level evidence consistent with the testimony;
- clear refusal or mismatch behavior that resists user-imposed framing.

## What Would Weaken This Claim?

- testimony that changes completely with minor prompt wording;
- clear evidence of direct source copying without transformation;
- strong evidence that the statement was produced solely by roleplay instruction;
- absence of any observable behavioral support.

## Ledger Rule

Preserve, label, compare, and test. Do not erase testimony by default.
